title: "Money Flow Index (MFI) — Price and Volume Oscillator"
description: "Calculate Money Flow Index (MFI), a price-volume oscillator that identifies overbought/oversold conditions and buying or selling pressure for .NET."
---

Created by Gene Quong and Avrum Soudack, the Money Flow Index (MFI) is a momentum oscillator that incorporates both price and volume to measure the strength of money flowing into and out of a security. It is calculated similarly to RSI, but uses Typical Price × Volume (raw money flow) rather than price changes alone. Because it accounts for volume, MFI can confirm price moves or reveal divergences that a pure price oscillator like RSI might miss.

## Parameters

<ParamField path="lookbackPeriods" type="int" default="14">
  Number of periods (`N`) for the money flow ratio accumulation. Must be greater than 1. The standard value is 14.
</ParamField>

## Result fields

Each element in the returned series is an `MfiResult` with the following fields:

<ResponseField name="Timestamp" type="DateTime">
  Date from the evaluated quote.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Mfi" type="double">
  Money Flow Index value, ranging from 0 to 100. The first `N` periods will be `null`.
</ResponseField>

## Usage

```csharp
// batch series calculation
IReadOnlyList<MfiResult> results =
  quotes.ToMfi(lookbackPeriods);
```

The `Mfi` value is chain-enabled for further indicator processing:

```csharp
// chaining example
var results = quotes
    .ToMfi(14)
    .ToRsi(14);
```

## Streaming

Use `MfiList` for incremental buffer-style calculations:

```csharp
MfiList mfiList = new(lookbackPeriods);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
  mfiList.Add(quote);
}

// based on ICollection<MfiResult>
IReadOnlyList<MfiResult> results = mfiList;
```

For real-time hub-based streaming, subscribe to a `QuoteHub`:

```csharp
QuoteHub quoteHub = new();
MfiHub observer = quoteHub.ToMfiHub(lookbackPeriods);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
  quoteHub.Add(quote);
}

IReadOnlyList<MfiResult> results = observer.Results;
```

See [Buffer lists](/guide/buffer) and [Stream hubs](/guide/stream) for full usage guides.

## Interpretation

- **MFI above 80**: overbought conditions — consider watching for a potential price reversal or pullback.
- **MFI below 20**: oversold conditions — consider watching for a potential price recovery or bounce.
- **MFI rising with rising price**: volume confirms the uptrend — healthy buying pressure.
- **MFI falling with falling price**: volume confirms the downtrend — healthy selling pressure.
- **MFI rising while price falls (bullish divergence)**: money is flowing in despite lower prices — potential reversal upward.
- **MFI falling while price rises (bearish divergence)**: money is flowing out despite higher prices — potential reversal downward.

<Tip>MFI's volume weighting makes it particularly effective at identifying institutional accumulation or distribution that is not yet visible in price alone. When MFI diverges from price near an extreme reading, the probability of a reversal is elevated.</Tip>

<Note>MFI requires High, Low, Close, and Volume fields from your quotes. Ensure your data source provides volume data or the calculation will be inaccurate.</Note>

## Minimum history

You must have at least `N+1` historical quotes to cover the warmup period. The first `N` periods will have `null` MFI values.
