---
title: "Bollinger Bands: Volatility Price Channels"
description: "Bollinger Bands plot standard deviation envelopes around an SMA to identify volatility expansions, squeeze setups, and mean-reversion opportunities."
---

Created by John Bollinger, [Bollinger Bands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bollinger_Bands) measure market volatility by placing upper and lower bands a configurable number of standard deviations away from a simple moving average of price. When the bands widen, volatility is expanding; when they contract into a squeeze, a breakout move is often imminent. Bollinger Bands® is a registered trademark of John A. Bollinger.

## Parameters

<ParamField path="lookbackPeriods" type="int" default="20">
  Number of periods (`N`) for the SMA center line. Must be greater than 1. A statistically meaningful sample — typically 20 — is strongly recommended.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="standardDeviations" type="double" default="2">
  Band width expressed as standard deviations (`D`) from the moving average. Must be greater than 0.
</ParamField>

## Result fields

Each element in the returned series is a `BollingerBandsResult` with the following fields.

<ResponseField name="Timestamp" type="DateTime">
  Date corresponding to the evaluated quote.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Sma" type="double">
  Simple moving average of price over `N` periods — the center line.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="UpperBand" type="double">
  `D` standard deviations above the SMA.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="LowerBand" type="double">
  `D` standard deviations below the SMA.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="PercentB" type="double">
  Location of price within the bands: `(Price - LowerBand) / (UpperBand - LowerBand)`. Values above 1 or below 0 indicate price outside the bands.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="ZScore" type="double">
  Number of standard deviations the current price is from the mean.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Width" type="double">
  Band width as a fraction of the SMA: `(UpperBand - LowerBand) / Sma`. Useful for measuring squeeze conditions.
</ResponseField>

## Usage

Call `ToBollingerBands()` on any `IEnumerable<TQuote>` to compute the full series in a single batch pass.

```csharp
IReadOnlyList<BollingerBandsResult> results =
    quotes.ToBollingerBands(lookbackPeriods, standardDeviations);
```

Bollinger Bands can also be computed from a transformed price source or chained with another indicator on the `PercentB` output:

```csharp
// compute bands on HL2 midpoint price
var results = quotes
    .Use(CandlePart.HL2)
    .ToBollingerBands(20, 2);

// chain RSI onto the %B signal
var rsiOfPercentB = quotes
    .ToBollingerBands(20, 2)
    .ToRsi(14);
```

<Tip>
  See [Chaining indicators](/guide/batch#chaining-indicators) for the full guide on composing indicator pipelines.
</Tip>

## Streaming

Use `BollingerBandsList` when you need incremental results without a hub — for example, processing a live feed one quote at a time.

```csharp
BollingerBandsList bbList = new(lookbackPeriods, standardDeviations);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
    bbList.Add(quote);
}

// read as IReadOnlyList<BollingerBandsResult>
IReadOnlyList<BollingerBandsResult> results = bbList;
```

For pub/sub architectures, subscribe an observer to a `QuoteHub`:

```csharp
QuoteHub quoteHub = new();
BollingerBandsHub observer =
    quoteHub.ToBollingerBandsHub(lookbackPeriods, standardDeviations);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
    quoteHub.Add(quote);
}

IReadOnlyList<BollingerBandsResult> results = observer.Results;
```

<Note>
  See [Buffer lists](/guide/buffer) and [Stream hubs](/guide/stream) for complete streaming guides.
</Note>

## Interpretation

Bollinger Bands do not generate directional signals on their own — they contextualize price relative to recent volatility:

- **Touch or breach of the upper band** suggests the market is relatively overbought; combined with a reversal candlestick it can signal a short entry.
- **Touch or breach of the lower band** suggests oversold conditions and a potential long setup.
- **%B > 1** means price is above the upper band; **%B < 0** means price is below the lower band.
- **Width squeeze** (narrow bands after a prolonged period of low volatility) often precedes a significant directional breakout.
- **Walking the bands** — when price repeatedly touches or exceeds one band — indicates a strong trend and should not be faded immediately.

<Tip>
  Combine with a momentum indicator such as RSI or MACD to filter false reversal signals at the bands.
</Tip>

## Minimum history

You must supply at least `N` periods of quotes. For statistically sound results, use a full lookback window well beyond the warm-up period — the library suggests `N` periods minimum, but more is always better.
