---
title: "STARC Bands: SMA Channels Scaled by ATR"
description: "STARC Bands place ATR-scaled channels around an SMA, defining low-risk entry zones and high-risk exhaustion levels for swing traders and mean-reversion setups."
---

Created by Manning Stoller, the [Stoller Average Range Channel (STARC) Bands](https://www.investopedia.com/terms/s/starc.asp) combine a Simple Moving Average center line with band widths derived from the Average True Range (ATR). The SMA provides a stable, non-lagging reference for the trend, while ATR scaling ensures the channel adapts to current volatility. STARC Bands give traders a sense of where price is "cheap" or "expensive" relative to the prevailing trend at any given volatility regime. See [Keltner Channels](/indicators/channels/keltner) for the EMA-centered equivalent.

## Parameters

<ParamField path="smaPeriods" type="int">
  Number of lookback periods (`S`) for the SMA center line. Must be greater than 1. Typically set between 5 and 10 for swing-trading applications.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="multiplier" type="double" default="2">
  ATR multiplier controlling band width. Must be greater than 0.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="atrPeriods" type="int" default="10">
  Number of lookback periods (`A`) for the Average True Range. Must be greater than 1. Commonly set equal to `smaPeriods`.
</ParamField>

## Result fields

Each element in the returned series is a `StarcBandsResult` with the following fields.

<ResponseField name="Timestamp" type="DateTime">
  Date corresponding to the evaluated quote.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="UpperBand" type="double">
  Upper STARC band: `SMA + (multiplier × ATR)`.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="Centerline" type="double">
  Simple moving average of price over `S` periods.
</ResponseField>

<ResponseField name="LowerBand" type="double">
  Lower STARC band: `SMA - (multiplier × ATR)`.
</ResponseField>

## Usage

Call `ToStarcBands()` on any `IEnumerable<TQuote>` to compute the full series in a single batch pass.

```csharp
IReadOnlyList<StarcBandsResult> results =
    quotes.ToStarcBands(smaPeriods, multiplier, atrPeriods);
```

<Note>
  STARC Bands are not chain-enabled. They must be generated from `quotes` directly and their result cannot be piped into further chain-enabled indicators.
</Note>

See [Chaining indicators](/guide/batch#chaining-indicators) for background on the chaining model.

## Streaming

Use `StarcBandsList` for incremental, event-driven processing without a hub:

```csharp
StarcBandsList starcBandsList = new(smaPeriods, multiplier, atrPeriods);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
    starcBandsList.Add(quote);
}

// read as IReadOnlyList<StarcBandsResult>
IReadOnlyList<StarcBandsResult> results = starcBandsList;
```

For pub/sub architectures, subscribe an observer to a `QuoteHub`:

```csharp
QuoteHub quoteHub = new();
StarcBandsHub observer =
    quoteHub.ToStarcBandsHub(smaPeriods, multiplier, atrPeriods);

foreach (IQuote quote in quotes)  // simulating stream
{
    quoteHub.Add(quote);
}

IReadOnlyList<StarcBandsResult> results = observer.Results;
```

<Note>
  See [Buffer lists](/guide/buffer) and [Stream hubs](/guide/stream) for complete streaming guides.
</Note>

## Interpretation

STARC Bands are designed to define high-probability entry and exit zones:

- **Price near the upper band** signals an area of high risk for new long entries — price is stretched above normal volatility relative to the trend.
- **Price near the lower band** signals a low-risk buying opportunity in an uptrend, where price has pulled back to an ATR-adjusted support level.
- **Upper band acts as a stop target** for short positions; lower band for long positions.
- **Price outside the bands** is not necessarily a trend reversal — it may indicate unusual momentum. Wait for confirmation before fading the move.
- Because the center line is a simple (non-lagging) average, STARC Bands react faster than Keltner Channels during sudden price changes.

<Tip>
  STARC Bands work well as a companion to a trend filter such as a 200-period SMA. Trade only the low-risk band touches in the direction confirmed by the longer trend filter.
</Tip>

## Minimum history

You must supply at least the greater of `S` or `A+100` periods of quotes to cover the warm-up and convergence periods. For better precision, provide at least `A+150` data points prior to the intended start date.

<Warning>
  The first `A+150` periods will contain convergence-related precision errors up to ~5% deviation in indicator values due to ATR smoothing warm-up. Use `.RemoveWarmupPeriods()` to trim these values before production use.
</Warning>
